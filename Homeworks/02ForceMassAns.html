  2. Critique the following statement. "On the moon, your mass is roughly 1/6 of what it would be on Earth due to light lunar gravity."

Your mass doesn't change. Your weight does.

  1. Alan Shepard famously drove a golf ball on the moon as a science demonstration (Apollo 14).

    How did the 1/6 lighter lunar gravity affect the speed of the ball as it left his club during the swing, vs the speed on Earth? Explain your answer.

No effect. The force of the club on the ball would be the same, for the same time and the ball's mass is the same. Gravity is irrelevant.
