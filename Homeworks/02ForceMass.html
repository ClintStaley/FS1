Homework on Masses and Forces

Unless otherwise indicated, use 10m/s210 m/s^210m/s2 for g in the following work. Also, no credit is given for merely correct answers. Show the work that arrives at your response. When relevant, round answers to 3 digits.

Basic

  1. Jim steps on a scale that measures his weight as 80kg. Jim is a stickler for proper labelling, and mutters that no scale should be measuring weight in "kg" or in "pounds" for that matter. He rewrites the 80kg mark on the scale as what value instead? (Despite being a stickler, Jim believes terrestrial gravity is 10m/s210 m/s^210m/s2)

  2. Critique the following statement. "On the moon, your mass is roughly 1/6 of what it would be on Earth due to light lunar gravity."

  3. Alan Shepard famously drove a golf ball on the moon as a science demonstration (Apollo 14).

    How did the 1/6 g lunar gravity affect the speed of the ball as it left his club during the swing, vs the speed on Earth? Explain your answer.

    a. Describe the direction and magnitude (up is positive), in proper units, of the two forces operating on you during the first 5 seconds and the acceleration that results, using Newton's 2nd law.

    b. How fast is the elevator rising in the middle portion with 50kg on the scale, what are the forces on you, and how does the speed change during that time?

    c. What are the forces on you and your acceleration during the x seconds? Given that the doors opened to a stationary floor, what is x?

    d. Despite being in a closed box, you can tell exactly how many meters upward you went. How many?
