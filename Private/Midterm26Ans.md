## 4. 15pts 
A car moving 30 m/s goes through a curve of radius 180m on a flat road, without skidding.  What is the minimum coefficient of static friction of tires on the road needed for this?  

**$\frac{30^2}{180} = 5 m/s^2$ acceleration (10pts), or .5G.  Ratio of downward to sideward force is thus .5, and that is the needed coefficient of friction (5pts)**

Draw the FBD for the block, and show the three forces on it, and their values.  Only one is known initially, but the other two can be deduced from it.

    b. How does the mass of the block factor into your final answer?  How does it affect the needed coefficient, in other words?

## 6. 30pts 
a. If water falls over a tall 100m waterfall, and assuming no other effects e.g. air resistance, how many degrees C does it heat as a result of the fall?  (Correct value is barely perceptible by touch.)  

b. Would some other liquid, say olive oil, or alchohol, see the same heating due to such a fall? If it would see the same rise in temperature, explain why.  If not, what factor(s) make a difference, and would it rise more or less than water in temperature, do you think?

**a: $100m(10J/m kg) = 1000 J/kg (8pts)$.  Given $4184 J/kg \degree C$, it heats by 1000/4184 = 0.239 \degree C$ (10pts)**

**b: Different, but not due to density since the heat calculation is per kg not volume (4pts).  But would change due to specific heat (4pts).  Almost all substances have lower specific heat than water, and thus we would expect them to rise *more* in temperature for the same energy (4pts)**


## 7. 18pts 
a. A speaker at the center of a plastic sphere of 4m radius emits a total 2.01W of sound energy.  What is the volume, in dB, of the sound from the speaker as it hits the sphere's internal wall?

b. The plastic sphere is 10cm thick, with a thermal conductivity of $0.5 \frac{W m}{m^2 \degree C}$  The outside temperature is 20C.  How warm will the inside be, due to the sound energy being emitted by the speaker?  Assume the sound energy is fully absorbed by the plastic sphere.  The result should be a very small amount of heating.

**a: Internal surface of the sphere: $2 \tau 4^2 = 201m^2$ (3pts).  Audio wattage per area: $2.01/201 = .01\frac{W}{m^2} = 100dB$ (7pts)**

**b: $.5 = \frac{.01 .1}{D}; D = \frac{.01 .1}{.5} = .002 \degree C$ (8pts), so 20.002C.**
