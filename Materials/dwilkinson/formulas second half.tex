\underline{Formulas from second half (not necessarily exhaustive)}

%\bigskip

Charge on an electron $e=-1.6\times 10^{-19}$ C

Coulomb constant $k=9\times 10^9$ Nm$^2$/C$^2$ (also note: $k=\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_{0}}$)

$\epsilon_0=8.85 \times 10^{-12}$ C$^2$/Nm$^2$

Force between two charges $F=\frac{kQq}{r^2}$

Electric field (magnitude) from a single charge, or spherical distribution $E=\frac{kQ}{r^2}$

Electric field (magnitude) between two sheets of charge $E=\frac{Q}{\epsilon_0 A}$

Potential energy near a single charge, or spherical distribution $U=\frac{kQq}{r}$

Voltage near a single charge, or spherical distribution $V=\frac{kQ}{r}$ 

Voltage difference in constant electric field $\Delta V=Ed$

Ohm's Law $V=IR$

Power delivered by a battery $P=IV$

Magnitude of magnetic force on moving charge $F=qvB\sin \theta$

Direction of magnetic force on moving charge: Right hand rule with thumb in direction of $\vec{v}$, fingers in direction of $\vec{B}$; opposite for negative charge

Magnitude of magnetic force on current $F=BIL \sin \theta$

Direction of magnetic force on current: Right hand rule with thumb in direction of $\vec{I}$, fingers in direction of $\vec{B}$

Magnitude of magnetic field near a long straight wire $B=\frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}$

$\mu_0=4\pi\times10^{-7}$ Tm/A

Magnitude of magnetic field inside a solenoid: $B=\frac{\mu_0 N I}{L}$

Voltage in a generator coil $V=\omega NBA \sin \omega t$

In AC, relation between maximum $V_{max}$ and $V_{rms}$, the ``root mean square" voltage: $V_{max}=V_{rms}\sqrt{2}$
